Everything from wildlife parks to championship golf courses to the historic King Shaka Route.

Ballito Golf

The Dolphin Coast and surrounding areas boast some of the finest golfing turfs in South Africa. These magnificent golf courses are both challenging and unforgettably beautiful, with unique settings and breathtaking views.

  • Prince's Grant
  • Simbithi Country Club
  • Zimbali Country Club
  • Umhlali Country Club

King Shaka Heritage Route

Located in Ballito, this cultural site follows in the footsteps of the great Zulu Chief King Shaka Zulu:

  • High Rock – Where King Shaka sat and planned his military tactics
  • Observation Rock – Where he watched army maneuvers on the flatland below
  • King Shaka's Spring – Where his handmaidens drew water
  • King Shaka's Pool and Cave – Where he bathed and rested

Ernie Els Signature Course

The Ernie Els Signature Course at Zimbali Lakes in KwaZulu-Natal is an 18-hole course designed by Ernie Els himself in collaboration with Golf Data. It's known for blending strategic design with stunning coastal and lake views, offering a challenging and rewarding experience for golfers of all levels.

Ballito Beaches

The beach is the main attraction for most visitors to KwaZulu Natal, and the Dolphin Coast has a magnificent stretch of golden beaches and sheltered coves ideal for a day of fun in the sun. From swimming beaches protected by shark nets and attended by professional Life Savers, to popular surfing spots and tidal pools, the Dolphin Coast's beaches have it all.

Dolphin Coast

The Dolphin Coast offers visitors a combination of modern facilities at its sun drenched seaside resorts in a lush, subtropical environment. The Dolphin Coast shoreline has exciting beaches and rock pools for safe bathing, surfing and scuba diving, rock and skiboat fishing, or just relaxing on the beach.

Moses Mabhida Big Swing

The 'Big Rush Big Swing' at the Moses Mabhida Stadium is a must. Jump into the void 106 meters above the World Cup football pitch and swing out in a huge 220 meter arc under the iconic arch. All equipment and instruction is provided by fully qualified staff.

The Battle of eNdondakusuka

eNdondakusuka was part of a civil war fought between the forces of Prince Cetshwayo and his brother Mbuyazi, for the right to be king of the Zulus in 1856. Some 15 000 to 25 000 warriors died in this battle, which established Cetshwayo as the king of the Zulus. The site, near the northern bank of the Thukela River, can be viewed from Fort Pearson on the opposite bank.

Hluhluwe-uMfolozi Game Park

The Dolphin Coast is close enough to Zululand to take a drive north and explore the African game reserves. The Hluhluwe-uMfolozi Game Park is approximately 257km north of Ballito. Most of the big game species including lion, cheetah, leopard, elephant, white and black rhinoceros, buffalo, and giraffe live in the reserve. Apart from the game viewing drives, there are also two self-guided auto trails.
